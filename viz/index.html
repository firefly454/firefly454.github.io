I really enjoy learning new ways to visualize data, from using ggplot in Python or R to even d3.js and Tableau. Here are a few things I've worked on, mainly for fun. If you have any comments or questions, please email me at e.kim AT berkeley DOT edu.

Global Life Expectancy vs. Fertility

I wanted to explore how life expectancy (in years) and fertility (in births per woman) change over time and across different countries. Using data from GapMinder.com and Tableau, I created the following dashboard. This dashboard allows you to change the range of years (from 1900 to 2015), to see how average life expectancy and fertility change for each country, in the top two figures. In the bottom figure, you can select an individual country (or "all" to get an average across all countries) to see a plot of fertility vs. life expectancy. On any of these figures, hover over a specific data point or country to see a small text box describing what is plotted.

In general, we can see that fertility decreases and life expectancy increases from 1900 to 2015. However, there are definite exceptions to this general trend. Look at Russia, for example - in 1900, the life expectancy was 30.75 years. By the year 1943, however, the life expectancy dramatically dropped to 15.83 years! Feel free to explore other countries and other ranges of years.
